Before submitting each writing assignment to me, you will submit it to your classmates for review. Your reviews of your classmates' writing are worth 10% of your final grade. Here are some tips for earning a good grade on peer reviewing:

  1. The minimum expectation is a 250-500 word comment (more is better) on your classmate's paper. If providing your classmate with track changes is also helpful, I encourage you to do so, but this is optional.
  2. Think of your classmate's paper as a collection of sentences, paragraphs, and ideas, not as a whole. The paper is not yet “good” or “bad,” but parts of it are one or the other. Which parts? Praise the good and give advice for the bad.
  3. Questions you might try to answer:
    • Is the paper responding to the assignment?
    • Is there information in the paper that is out of place? Or is there information that is not included but should be?
    • Is high-priority information given low priority, or vice versa?
    • Could passages of the paper be more clear?
  4. Grammar, spelling, etc. should not be your focus. Prioritize ideas and communication quality. You should only very rarely comment on the mechanics of your classmate's writing.
